Positive Degree Days (PDD)

Positive Degree Days quantify melt potential by summing daily average temperatures above 0 °C. This Colab notebook demonstrates how to calculate PDD using reanalysis temperature fields accessed via the Copernicus Climate Change Service (C3S) API.

  • Calculate daily averages & PDD time series

KMeans‑Derived Pseudo‑Labels

This Machine learning (ML) pipeline uses PCA and K‑Means clustering to automatically generate pseudo‑labels for Sentinel‑2 imagery. Random Forest and SVM classifiers then identify snow, ice and ground pixels, enabling snowline detection and Snow Cover Ratio computation.

  • Download and preprocess satellite scenes
  • Train on unsupervised pseudo‑labels & classify terrain
  • Extract snow‑ice boundaries and calculate snowline altitudes

Datasets

Glacier Shapefiles & Centre Lines

Outlines and centerlines for ten glaciers — Caribou, Fork Beard, Fountain, Highway, Nakarpog, Nerutusoq, Norman, Rundle, Sermilik, and Turner — have been prepared for the years 1958/59 and 2023 within Auyuittuq and Sirmilik National Parks. These datasets have been submitted to the Randolph Glacier Inventory (RGI) to support long-term glacier monitoring and change analysis.

Annual Maximum Summer Snowline Dataset (2013–2024)

Cheung (2025) curated annual maximum summer snowline elevations for six glaciers in the Canadian Arctic. Snowlines were derived using Snow‑Elevation Histogram Analysis (SEHA) and binned Accumulation Area Ratio methods from Landsat 8/9 and Sentinel‑2 imagery. Fields include dates, snowline altitudes, snow cover area and accumulation area ratio metrics.

Glaciers: Highway, Bylot D20s, Coronation, Fountain, Turner and White.
